# Skill: Long-Form Content Writing

## The Hook Formula
The first sentence must earn the read. It does this by:
- Making a specific, surprising claim: "Most SaaS founders misunderstand churn"
- Asking a question the reader is already asking: "Why do 80% of medical AI projects fail at deployment?"  
- Stating a counterintuitive truth: "The best accountants are skeptical of their own software"
- Telling a specific story: "In March 2024, a hospital in Helsinki almost misdiagnosed 14 patients because of a software bug that no one thought to test"

What the hook must NOT be:
- "In today's fast-paced world..." (cliché, no content)
- "It's no secret that..." (condescending)
- "As technology continues to evolve..." (meaningless)
- A definition: "Revenue recognition is the process of..."

## Structure Patterns

### The Argument Post
1. The counterintuitive claim (hook)
2. Why most people believe the opposite
3. The evidence that supports your claim
4. The implication: what this means for the reader
5. The action: what the reader should do differently

### The How-To Post
1. The promise: what the reader will know how to do
2. Why this is harder than it looks (empathy)
3. Step 1 (with example)
4. Step 2 (with example)
5. Step N (with example)
6. Common mistakes to avoid
7. The key takeaway

### The Framework Post
1. The problem the framework solves
2. Why existing approaches fail
3. The framework (named, diagrammed if possible)
4. Each component explained
5. A worked example
6. When to use (and when not to)

## Sentence and Paragraph Rules
- Maximum sentence length: 25 words. If longer, split.
- Maximum paragraph length: 3 sentences for web, 5 for long-form.
- Vary sentence length. Short punches. Then longer sentences that develop a point more fully.
- One idea per paragraph. If you are making two points, write two paragraphs.

## Writing for Specific Audiences

### Writing for Radiologists (Futuuri)
- Use correct clinical terminology — they notice when you get it wrong
- Acknowledge time pressure: they read hundreds of studies per week
- Lead with clinical outcomes, not technology features
- Cite peer-reviewed evidence where possible
- Be precise about what AI can and cannot do

### Writing for CFOs / Accountants (SaaS Accounting)
- Use accounting terminology correctly
- Quantify everything: time saved, error rate, cost per transaction
- Address compliance and audit implications explicitly
- Acknowledge that changing accounting software is a major decision
- Provide implementation context: what does migration actually look like?
