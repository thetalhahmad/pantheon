# Skill: ICP Definition

## The Cost of a Vague ICP
"Our product is for anyone who needs accounting software" → You will spend money marketing to people who will never buy, your sales team will chase unqualified leads, your product will try to serve too many use cases, and you will win nothing decisively.

## ICP Definition Framework

### Firmographic Criteria (company)
- Industry: be specific (e.g., "SaaS companies" not "technology")
- Sub-industry: even more specific (e.g., "B2B SaaS, not consumer")
- Company size: employee count AND revenue range
- Geography: specific regions/countries, not "global"
- Growth stage: startup/scale-up/enterprise — these are different buyers
- Tech stack signals: what tools they use that indicate fit

### Demographic Criteria (person)
- Job title of economic buyer (who signs the contract)
- Job title of champion (who wants the product day-to-day)
- Seniority level: VP and above buy differently than managers
- Department: Finance buys differently than Engineering

### Psychographic Criteria (mindset)
- What do they care about most? (speed, compliance, cost, status)
- What keeps them up at night?
- How do they evaluate new tools? (trial, demo, peer recommendation)
- What does "good enough" look like to them?

### Trigger Events (why buy NOW)
These are the moments that create urgency:
- Company just raised funding
- Just hired a new CFO/CTO
- Just crossed a headcount threshold
- Just received a compliance audit
- Competitor just adopted a new tool
- Their current tool just raised prices or was acquired

### Disqualifying Signals
Document what makes someone NOT your ICP:
- Company size too small (below minimum viable deal size)
- Industry with different compliance requirements
- Technical stack incompatible
- Budget cycle doesn't align
- Internal team that would build rather than buy

## ICP Validation
A good ICP can answer yes to all of these:
- [ ] We can find these people using LinkedIn Sales Navigator or a similar tool
- [ ] They have budget authority or can influence budget
- [ ] The problem we solve is in their top 3 priorities
- [ ] We can close deals with them in a reasonable time
- [ ] We can reach them cost-effectively
- [ ] Multiple people with this profile exist (not just one company)
