# Skill: Pitch Deck Design

## The Rule of One
Every slide has ONE job. One idea. One message.
If you need two ideas on a slide, you need two slides.

## The Narrative Arc
A pitch deck tells a story. The structure is:
1. **Setup** — here is how the world works today (problem)
2. **Conflict** — here is why that is not good enough (urgency)
3. **Resolution** — here is what we have built (solution + product)
4. **Evidence** — here is proof it works (traction)
5. **Vision** — here is what becomes possible (market + future)
6. **Ask** — here is what we need to get there (the ask)

## Slide-by-Slide Guidelines

### Slide 1: Problem
Goal: Make the audience feel the pain before you show anything

Structure:
- One sentence stating the problem
- 2-3 data points that prove it is a real, large problem
- Optionally: a story or scenario that makes it vivid

What not to do:
- Do not introduce your company on this slide
- Do not mention your solution on this slide
- Do not make it too broad — "healthcare is broken" is not a problem, "radiologists spend 40% of their time on administrative tasks instead of reading studies" is

### Slide 2: Solution
Goal: The insight, not the product

The insight is the non-obvious idea that makes your approach different:
"Everyone builds AI to detect findings. We build AI to prioritize them — because detection is not the bottleneck, cognitive load is."

The insight comes before you show the product. If you show the product before the insight, you make it look like a feature, not a solution.

### Slide 3: Product
Goal: Show it working, do not describe it

Use a screenshot or a 30-second demo gif. Never a feature list.

Caption should say what the user achieves, not what the feature is:
✓ "Radiologists see critical findings first, every time"
✗ "AI-powered finding prioritization dashboard"

### Slide 4: Traction
The single most important slide in a fundraising deck.

Include your best number, prominently. Then context.

Format:
- Headline metric (large, centered): "€2.3M ARR"
- Growth chart (MRR over 12 months)
- 2-3 supporting metrics: customer count, NRR, notable customers

What counts as traction (in order of persuasiveness):
1. Revenue (especially with growth rate)
2. Signed contracts (even if not yet live)
3. Paid pilots
4. LOIs / signed MOUs
5. Active users (if free product with conversion path)
6. Waitlist signups (weakest — use only if nothing else)

### Slide 5: Market
Goal: Show this is worth building

The mistake: showing a massive TAM number without context.
"The global healthcare AI market is $45 billion by 2028" — investor has heard this 100 times.

Better approach: bottom-up market sizing.
"There are 8,000 radiology departments in Europe. Average deal size is €50K/year. That is a €400M addressable market in Europe alone. We are targeting the 800 departments with >5 radiologists — a €40M serviceable market."

Bottom-up > top-down. Specific > generic. Europe > "global."

### Slide 6: Business Model
Keep it simple. Answer:
- How do you make money? (subscription, usage-based, per-seat)
- What does a deal look like? (ACV, contract length)
- What are the unit economics? (LTV:CAC, payback period)

### Slide 7: Team
"We invest in people, not ideas."

For each founder:
- Name and role
- ONE relevant credential (the most relevant one, not all of them)
- The specific unfair advantage they bring

Example:
"Talha — CEO. Previously built AI diagnostics infrastructure at X. First-generation healthtech founder with direct clinical network."

### Slide 8: The Ask
Specific. Justified. Connected to milestones.

"We are raising €1.5M seed to:
- Hire 2 engineers + 1 clinical sales lead
- Reach €500K ARR in 12 months
- Complete MDR certification for EU Class IIa SaMD

This puts us on track for Series A at €2M+ ARR."

## Visual Design Rules

### Hierarchy
- Title: 28-36px, bold, high contrast
- Body: 20-24px, regular weight, secondary color
- Caption/footnote: 16-18px, muted color
- Never use font below 16px in a presentation

### Color
- Maximum 3 colors: brand primary, brand secondary, neutral
- Use color to highlight the one thing per slide that matters most
- Dark slides work for impact moments (big stats, quotes)
- Light slides for content-heavy slides

### Data visualization
- One chart per slide
- Remove chart junk: gridlines, borders, legends if labels are used
- Label the most important data point directly on the chart
- Y-axis should start at 0 for bar charts (never truncate to exaggerate growth)
