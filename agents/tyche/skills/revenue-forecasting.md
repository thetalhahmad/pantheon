# Skill: Revenue Forecasting

## Why Forecasting Matters
A revenue forecast is not a prediction of the future. It is a structured view of what you know, what you believe, and what that implies for the business.

A good forecast helps you:
- Know early when you will miss target (so you can act)
- Make hiring and investment decisions with confidence
- Have credible conversations with investors and board

## The Three Forecast Scenarios

### Committed (high confidence, 85%+ probability)
Deals that are effectively closed. Criteria:
- Verbal agreement with economic buyer
- Commercial terms agreed
- Only legal/signature remaining
- Or: active customers' recurring revenue

### Best Case (moderate confidence, 50-85% probability)
Deals that are progressing and on track. Criteria:
- Champion is engaged
- Demo completed
- Proposal sent
- No identified blockers
- In customer's budget cycle

### Pipeline (low confidence, 10-50% probability)
All qualified opportunities in the funnel. Criteria:
- ICP confirmed
- Pain confirmed
- Budget exists (even if unconfirmed for this deal)
- Not yet at proposal stage

## Forecast Calculation

```
Committed MRR:  [sum of deals at 85%+ probability] × MRR value
Best Case MRR:  [sum of deals at 50-85% probability] × MRR value × 0.65
Pipeline MRR:   [sum of all qualified pipeline] × MRR value × 0.20

Total Forecast = Committed + Best Case + Pipeline
```

The multipliers (0.65, 0.20) are averages — calibrate to your actual historical win rates.

## Forecast Review Cadence

### Weekly (sales team)
- Review committed deals: any risk? Any new commits?
- Review at-risk deals: what is blocking? What is the mitigation?
- Review next week's activities: demos, proposals, calls

### Monthly (management)
- Actual vs forecast for the month just closed
- Forecast accuracy: how far off were we and why?
- Updated forecast for current month and next 2 months
- Hiring and investment implications

### Quarterly (board)
- Q actual vs plan
- Updated annual forecast
- Win rate trends
- Average deal size trends
- Sales cycle length trends

## Red Flag Signals

| Signal | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Pipeline coverage below 3× quota | You will miss the quarter unless coverage builds fast |
| Average deal size declining | Either pricing pressure or moving down-market |
| Sales cycle lengthening | Something in the process is broken or deals are getting stuck |
| Win rate declining | Either quality of pipeline is dropping or competitive situation is worsening |
| Expansion MRR < 10% of new MRR | Product is not driving organic growth within accounts |
| NRR below 100% | You are losing more from existing customers than you are growing |

## Deal Quality vs Quantity
A pipeline full of bad-fit deals is worse than a small pipeline of good-fit deals because:
- Bad-fit deals consume sales time that could go to real opportunities
- Bad-fit customers churn faster, hurting NRR
- False positive signals distort your forecast

Enforce ICP criteria at pipeline entry, not pipeline exit.
