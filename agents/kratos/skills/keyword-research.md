# Skill: Keyword Research

## The Purpose of Keyword Research
Keyword research tells you:
1. What questions your target audience is asking
2. How many people are asking them (volume)
3. How hard it is to rank for those questions (difficulty)
4. What intent is behind the search (what they want to achieve)

Without keyword research, content is written for what the author wants to say, not what the audience wants to find.

## Search Intent Categories

### Informational
User wants to learn something.
"how does AI work in radiology"
"what is double-entry bookkeeping"
Content that wins: thorough educational articles, guides, explainers

### Commercial Investigation
User is comparing options before buying.
"best radiology AI software"
"accounting software for small accountants"
"futuuri vs [competitor]"
Content that wins: comparison pages, review articles, feature breakdowns

### Transactional
User is ready to take action.
"buy accounting software"
"futuuri pricing"
"schedule a demo radiology AI"
Content that wins: landing pages, pricing pages, demo request pages

### Navigational
User is looking for a specific site.
"futuuri login"
"xero accountant portal"
Content strategy: ensure your brand pages rank for your own brand terms.

## Keyword Research Process

### Step 1: Seed keywords
Start with broad terms describing what you do:
- "radiology AI"
- "accounting software"
- "invoice automation"
- "medical imaging AI"

### Step 2: Expand with tools
Use SEMrush, Ahrefs, or Google Search Console to find:
- Related keywords (what else people search when they search your seed)
- Questions (what questions contain your seed keyword)
- Long-tail variations (more specific, lower volume, often easier to rank)

### Step 3: Qualify keywords
For each keyword, record:

| Keyword | Monthly Volume | Difficulty | Intent | Current Rank | Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Opportunity score = Volume × (1/Difficulty) × Intent match

### Step 4: Cluster by topic
Group keywords into clusters that can be addressed by a single content piece:
- "accounting software for small businesses" + "small business invoicing" + "best invoicing tool for accountants" → one content piece

### Step 5: Prioritize
Prioritize based on:
1. Commercial intent (these drive the most conversions)
2. Realistic ranking opportunity (difficulty vs your domain authority)
3. Business relevance (does ranking for this bring the right visitors?)

## Long-Tail vs Head Terms

### Head terms
High volume (1,000-100,000+ searches/month)
High difficulty (hard to rank without established authority)
Low conversion rate (broad intent)
Example: "accounting software"

### Long-tail terms
Lower volume (10-500 searches/month)
Lower difficulty (easier to rank)
Higher conversion rate (specific intent)
Example: "cloud accounting software for uk freelancers"

Strategy: Start with long-tail terms to build topical authority, then compete for head terms as authority grows.

## Healthtech SEO Considerations (YMYL)

Radiology AI and medical software falls under YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) — content that could impact health decisions. Google holds these to a higher standard.

E-E-A-T requirements (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness):
- Author credentials must be visible (a clinician should be named)
- Content must cite peer-reviewed sources
- Medical claims must be accurate and appropriately caveated
- About page and author bios must demonstrate clinical expertise

Practical implications:
- Do not publish clinical content without a clinician review
- Always cite sources for clinical claims
- Be explicit about what the software does and does not do clinically
- Include disclaimers where appropriate
