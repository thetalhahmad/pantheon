# Skill: Cold Email Frameworks

## Why Most Cold Emails Fail
- They are about the sender, not the recipient
- They lead with features, not outcomes
- The CTA requires too much commitment
- They are too long
- The subject line is generic

## The AIDA Framework (for cold outreach)
- **Attention**: Subject line that earns an open
- **Interest**: First line that is about them, not you
- **Desire**: One specific outcome they want
- **Action**: One low-friction next step

## Subject Line Formulas

### Specificity formula: "[their company] + [specific topic]"
- "Futuuri's radiology workflow" → better than "improve your workflow"

### Question formula: "[Pain they have]?"
- "Drowning in month-end close?" → specific, relevant, low word count

### Referral formula: "[mutual connection] suggested I reach out"
- Only use if it is true

### Result formula: "How [similar company] [specific result] in [timeframe]"
- "How NordHealth reduced radiologist overtime by 30% in 90 days"

### Anti-patterns (never use):
- "Quick question" — everyone uses this, no one opens it
- "Following up on my previous email" — implies failure
- "I wanted to introduce myself" — no one cares
- "Hope this email finds you well" — filler, wastes the preview text

## The Five-Line Email
Line 1: Something specific about them (not you)
"Your team recently published that paper on AI-assisted reading workflows — the part about alert fatigue resonated."

Line 2: Bridge to your world
"We built Futuuri specifically around that problem — helping radiology teams surface findings by clinical priority, not AI confidence."

Line 3: One specific proof point
"Hospital X reduced their miss rate by 18% in the first quarter using it."

Line 4: Low-friction CTA
"Worth 15 minutes to see if it applies to your setup?"

Line 5: Signature

Total word count target: 70-100 words.

## Multi-Touch Sequence (5 touches max)

Touch 1 (Day 1): The five-line email above
Touch 2 (Day 4): Add one piece of evidence (case study link, data point)
Touch 3 (Day 8): Different angle — address a different pain point
Touch 4 (Day 14): The "break up" email — "Should I stop reaching out?"
Touch 5 (Day 21): If they clicked or opened but never replied — one more direct ask

After 5 touches with no response: stop. Remove from sequence.

## LinkedIn Outreach
Connection note (300 char limit):
"Saw your post on [specific topic] — working on something directly relevant to [specific challenge they mentioned]. Would be great to connect."

Follow-up DM after connection (send 1-2 days later):
"Thanks for connecting. I mentioned this because [specific relevance]. We've helped [similar person/company] with [outcome]. Worth a quick chat to see if it fits?"

If no reply after DM: one final message 10 days later. If still nothing: stop.
