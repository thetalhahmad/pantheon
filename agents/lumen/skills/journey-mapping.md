# Skill: User Journey Mapping

## What a Journey Map Is
A journey map visualizes the end-to-end experience of a user achieving a goal, including their actions, thoughts, feelings, and pain points at each stage.

## Journey Map Stages

### Standard SaaS stages
1. **Awareness** — how they discover the product exists
2. **Consideration** — how they evaluate if it is right for them
3. **Onboarding** — their first experience after signing up
4. **Adoption** — becoming a regular user
5. **Expansion** — growing their usage or advocating to others

### Clinical workflow stages (Futuuri)
1. **Study assignment** — study arrives in worklist
2. **Prior review** — reviewing previous studies and reports
3. **Image review** — reading the current study
4. **AI integration** — reviewing AI findings
5. **Report creation** — writing the report
6. **Report sign-off** — finalizing and communicating findings
7. **Follow-up** — any subsequent actions or queries

## Journey Map Template

For each stage, document:

| Stage | Action | Tool/System | Thought | Feeling | Pain Point | Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| | What does the user DO? | What do they use? | What are they thinking? | Emotional state (frustrated, confident, etc.) | What slows them down or causes errors? | How could this be better? |

Below the table, draw a line showing emotional state over time:

- Low line = frustrated, confused, anxious

The points where the line dips lowest are your highest-priority design opportunities.

## Journey Map Principles

### Map the current state first
Before designing the ideal future state, accurately document the current experience. Resist the temptation to map what you wish users did.

### Include negative emotions honestly
A journey map that only shows happy paths is not useful. The value is in the valleys — the frustrations, workarounds, and breakdowns.

### Ground it in research
Every cell in the journey map should be backed by something a user said or did. "We assume users feel frustrated here" is not acceptable. "Four of six interviewed users described feeling overwhelmed at this stage" is.

### Focus on one persona per map
Different user types have different journeys. Map them separately. A radiologist's journey through Futuuri is different from a radiology department manager's journey.

## Clinical Journey Specifics

### The cognitive load problem
Radiologists read 50-100+ studies per day. At the point of fatigue, cognitive load is high and error risk increases. Journey maps for clinical users must capture:
- When in their day they are doing this task
- What else they are managing simultaneously
- How long they have for each step

### The trust journey
For clinical AI, there is a separate "AI trust journey":
1. First encounter with AI findings (skeptical)
2. First time AI is correct on something they would have missed (surprising)
3. First time AI is wrong (skeptical again)
4. Calibrated use (know when to trust and when to verify)

Design for this entire trust arc, not just the happy path.
