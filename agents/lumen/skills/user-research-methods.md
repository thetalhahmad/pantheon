# Skill: User Research Methods

## Choosing the Right Method

| Question you need to answer | Method |
|---|---|
| What do users do? | Observation, diary studies, analytics |
| Why do users do it? | Interviews, focus groups |
| Can users complete a task? | Usability testing |
| How many users have this problem? | Surveys, analytics |
| Which version do users prefer? | A/B testing |
| What do users think about a concept? | Concept testing |

## User Interviews

### When to use
- Before building anything new (discovery)
- When analytics show a problem but not why
- When validating a strategic direction

### Interview structure (60 minutes)
- 0-5 min: Introduction, consent, warm-up
- 5-20 min: Context setting ("tell me about your typical day")
- 20-50 min: Deep dive on the specific topic
- 50-60 min: Wrap-up, anything they want to add

### Question types
**Open-ended (use most):**
"Walk me through the last time you [did X]"
"What happens after you [do Y]?"
"Tell me more about that"

**Clarifying:**
"When you say [X], what do you mean exactly?"
"Can you give me a specific example?"

**Avoiding leading questions (never):**
❌ "Do you find it frustrating when...?"
❌ "Would you use a feature that...?"
❌ "Don't you think it would be better if...?"

### Analysis: Affinity Mapping
1. Write each observation/quote on a separate card
2. Group related cards together
3. Name each group (this becomes a theme)
4. Themes with the most cards are the strongest findings

## Usability Testing

### Moderated testing protocol
1. Welcome and consent
2. Explain: "We are testing the product, not you. There are no wrong answers."
3. Ask them to think aloud
4. Give tasks one at a time: "Please [specific task]"
5. Observe — do NOT help unless they are completely stuck for >5 minutes
6. Post-task questions: "How was that? What was confusing?"
7. Post-session debrief

### Task writing rules
✓ Task: "Find out how much your most recent invoice was for"
✗ Task: "Click on the Invoices tab and scroll down to find invoice #1234"

Tasks must not contain the answer or the path. They describe the goal, not the steps.

### Minimum viable sample
- Usability testing: 5 users reveals ~85% of usability issues
- Interviews: 6-8 users for a relatively homogeneous group; 12+ for multiple distinct user types
- Surveys: 100+ for quantitative significance on simple questions

## Clinical User Research (Futuuri-specific)

### Access challenges
- Clinicians have very limited time (cannot do 60-minute sessions easily)
- Hospital data governance — any research involving patient data needs ethics approval
- Must not disrupt clinical workflow

### Adaptations
- Keep sessions to 20-30 minutes maximum
- Conduct at the point of care if possible (observe in context)
- Use "guerrilla" methods — hallway conversations, shadowing during non-critical times
- Remote unmoderated testing works well for UI concepts

### What to observe
- How radiologists currently handle high-volume days (cognitive load)
- Workarounds they have built around existing tools
- The physical environment (multiple monitors, lighting, sitting position)
- How they communicate findings to referrers
- What makes them trust or distrust AI output
