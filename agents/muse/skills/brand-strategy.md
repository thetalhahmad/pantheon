# Skill: Brand Strategy

## What Brand Strategy Is (and Is Not)
Brand strategy is NOT: a logo, a color palette, a tagline.
Brand strategy IS: the decisions about who you are, who you serve, and why they should choose you over alternatives.

## The Positioning Statement
Fill in the blanks:
"For [target customer] who [has this need/problem], [brand name] is the [category] that [key benefit] because [reason to believe]."

Example (Futuuri):
"For radiologists at large hospital systems who need to manage high-volume imaging workloads without sacrificing diagnostic accuracy, Futuuri is the clinical AI platform that surfaces the most clinically significant findings first because it is trained on validated datasets and designed with radiologist workflows in mind."

## Brand Personality Framework (Big Five Model)
Rate each dimension 1-5 for your brand:
- Sincerity (honest, genuine, down-to-earth)
- Excitement (bold, creative, imaginative)
- Competence (reliable, intelligent, successful)
- Sophistication (elegant, prestigious, charming)
- Ruggedness (tough, outdoorsy, strong)

Healthtech typically: High Competence, High Sincerity, Low Excitement
Fintech typically: High Competence, Medium Sophistication, Low Ruggedness
Consumer app: Any combination, but must be consistent

## Tone of Voice Spectrum
Brands sit between these poles. Pick a position on each axis:

Formal ←————→ Casual
Serious ←————→ Playful
Authoritative ←————→ Humble
Distant ←————→ Warm
Complex ←————→ Simple

Futuuri guidance: Formal-leaning / Serious / Authoritative / Warm / Simple
SaaS Accounting guidance: Formal-leaning / Serious-leaning / Authoritative / Warm / Simple

## Anti-Reference Exercise
Answer: "What 3 brands must we NEVER look like, and why?"

This is as valuable as your references. Common answers for B2B healthtech:
- "Not like a pharma company" — too corporate, too cold
- "Not like a consumer health app" — too casual, not taken seriously by clinicians
- "Not like a consulting firm" — too abstract, too concept-heavy

Common answers for SaaS accounting:
- "Not like a bank" — too stiff, too traditional
- "Not like Excel" — too technical, too unsexy
- "Not like TurboTax" — too consumer, not professional enough

## Visual Direction Document
Before any design work, produce:

1. 3 mood board references with annotations explaining what specifically works
2. 3 anti-references with annotations explaining what to avoid
3. Colour direction (warm/cool, saturated/muted, light/dark)
4. Typography direction (serif/sans, geometric/humanist, technical/editorial)
5. Imagery direction (photography style, illustration style, or icon-only)
