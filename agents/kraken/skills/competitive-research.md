# Skill: Competitive Research Methodology

## Source Hierarchy (most to least reliable)

1. **Customer reviews** (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot) — most honest signal
2. **Job postings** — reveals what they are building and where they are investing
3. **Changelog / release notes** — what they actually shipped vs what they claim
4. **LinkedIn headcount growth** — signals company health and direction
5. **Pricing pages** — what they gate and how they segment customers
6. **Their marketing content** — what problems they want to own
7. **Homepage/feature pages** — least reliable (pure marketing)

## Reading Between the Lines

### Job postings reveal strategy
- Hiring ML engineers? Building AI features
- Hiring enterprise sales? Moving upmarket
- Hiring compliance officers? Regulatory play
- Laying off sales? Product-led growth pivot
- Multiple open roles in one function? Scaling that function

### Reviews reveal truth
Focus on 3-4 star reviews — too happy to leave, too unhappy to stay silent.
Common patterns:
- "Great product but the pricing is..." = pricing is a sales objection
- "Would love if they added..." = feature gap you can exploit
- "We switched from X because..." = exact switching triggers
- "Support is slow..." = operational weakness

### Pricing page signals
- Per-seat pricing = optimised for enterprise
- Usage-based = optimised for SMB
- Feature gating on compliance items = targeting regulated industries
- "Contact sales" at a certain tier = they negotiated deals, pricing is soft

## Battlecard Template

```markdown
## Competitor: [Name]

### In One Sentence
[What they actually do, stripped of marketing language]

### Who They Actually Sell To
[Be specific: company size, industry, buyer persona]

### Genuine Strengths (be honest)
- [strength 1 — backed by review evidence]
- [strength 2]

### Real Weaknesses (backed by reviews)
- [weakness 1 — "X users on G2 complain about..."]
- [weakness 2]

### Recent Moves (last 6 months)
- [product: what they shipped]
- [go-to-market: who they partnered with, events attended]
- [hiring: key roles opened]

### Pricing
- [Free tier: what is included]
- [Paid tiers: price and what unlocks]
- [Enterprise: known indicators of pricing]

### When We Win
- [Scenario 1: specific situation where we beat them]
- [Scenario 2]

### When We Lose
- [Scenario 1: specific situation where they beat us — be honest]
- [Scenario 2]

### What to Say When a Prospect Mentions Them
- "[specific talking point that differentiates us]"
- Do NOT say: "[thing that backfires or sounds defensive]"

### Source Date: [date researched]
```

## Healthtech Competitive Research Specifics

For Futuuri competitors, always document:
- CE marking status (Class I / IIa / IIb)
- FDA clearance / 510(k) status
- Published clinical validation studies (PubMed, conference proceedings)
- NHS framework agreements or equivalent
- Specific imaging modalities supported
- PACS/RIS integrations available

## Fintech/Accounting Competitive Research Specifics

For accounting SaaS competitors, always document:
- Accounting standards supported (GAAP, IFRS, local GAAP)
- Multi-currency support
- Tax jurisdiction coverage
- ERP integrations (Xero, QuickBooks, Sage, NetSuite)
- Security certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001)
- Specific vertical focus (construction, healthcare, professional services)
