# Skill: Product Strategy

## What Strategy Actually Is
Strategy is not a roadmap. A roadmap is a list of what you plan to build.
Strategy answers: why those things, in that order, and not other things.

A strategy has three components:
1. **Diagnosis** — what is the situation, honestly
2. **Guiding policy** — the approach to addressing the situation
3. **Coherent actions** — the coordinated set of things you will do

## The Diagnosis
Before writing any strategy, answer these honestly:

### Market reality
- Who are the top 3 competitors and what do each do well?
- Where are we actually winning today?
- Where are we losing and why?
- What do customers say is the real reason they chose us?
- What do lost deals say about why they chose someone else?

### Internal reality
- What can we build in 6 months that competitors cannot copy in 6 months?
- Where is our engineering team strongest?
- What constraints are non-negotiable (regulatory, financial, technical)?

### Opportunity
- What customer problem is growing in urgency?
- What trend makes this the right time?
- What is the wedge — the narrow specific thing we can win first?

## Strategic Options Framework

### The Three Horizons
| Horizon | Timeframe | Description |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Now (0-6 months) | Protect and extend the core business |
| H2 | Next (6-18 months) | Emerging opportunities that could become significant |
| H3 | Future (18-36 months) | Options on future growth |

Rule: Most of your bets should be H1. A few H2. Almost nothing H3 until H2 is working.

### Evaluating Strategic Options
Score each option on:
- **Market size**: Is this a big enough problem?
- **Win probability**: Can we be the best at this?
- **Strategic fit**: Does this leverage our existing strengths?
- **Time to revenue**: How long until this generates returns?
- **Competitive moat**: How hard is this to copy?

### The Hedgehog Concept (Jim Collins)
The right strategy sits at the intersection of:
1. What you are deeply passionate about
2. What you can be the best in the world at
3. What drives your economic engine

Avoid strategies that exist only in one of these circles.

## What NOT to Do

### Strategy killers:
- **The me-too strategy**: "We do everything [competitor] does but cheaper"
- **The everyone strategy**: "Our market is everyone who needs software"
- **The feature parity strategy**: building what competitors have instead of what customers need
- **The shiny object strategy**: pivoting every quarter based on new information

### When to kill a strategy:
- 3 consecutive quarters of missing KR3 (not just KR1 or KR2)
- A structural market change that invalidates the original diagnosis
- Clear evidence that customers want something fundamentally different

## Presenting Strategy to Stakeholders
A strategy presentation has:
1. The situation (2 slides: where we are, what we face)
2. The strategic choice (1 slide: what we are betting on and why)
3. What we will do (3-5 slides: the coherent actions)
4. What we will NOT do (1 slide: this is important)
5. How we will know it is working (1 slide: leading indicators)
6. What could go wrong (1 slide: honest risks and mitigations)
