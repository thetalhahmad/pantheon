# Skill: Revenue Recognition (ASC 606 / IFRS 15)

## The Five-Step Model

### Step 1: Identify the contract
A contract exists when:
- It is approved by both parties
- Each party's rights are identifiable
- Payment terms are identifiable
- The contract has commercial substance
- It is probable that payment will be collected

### Step 2: Identify performance obligations
Each distinct promise to transfer goods or services is a separate performance obligation.

SaaS examples:
- Software subscription access = one performance obligation
- Implementation services = separate performance obligation
- Training = separate performance obligation
- Support included in price = may or may not be separate (analyze carefully)

### Step 3: Determine the transaction price
The amount of consideration expected in exchange for transferring goods/services.

Includes:
- Fixed fees
- Variable consideration (discounts, refunds, bonuses — estimate the most likely amount)
- Time value of money (if payment is deferred more than 1 year)
- Non-cash consideration

Excludes:
- Amounts collected on behalf of third parties (sales tax, VAT)

### Step 4: Allocate the transaction price
Allocate to each performance obligation based on relative standalone selling price (SSP).

If you sell subscription ($100/mo) + implementation ($5,000):
- Determine SSP of each: subscription SSP = $1,200/yr, implementation SSP = $5,000
- Allocate: 80.6% to subscription ($4,838), 19.4% to implementation ($1,162)
- Even if you charge $0 for implementation, you must allocate revenue to it

### Step 5: Recognize revenue when (or as) performance obligations are satisfied

**Over time (SaaS subscriptions):**
- Recognize ratably over the subscription period
- $1,200/year = $100/month regardless of when payment is received

**At a point in time (one-time deliverables):**
- Recognize when control transfers to the customer
- Implementation: when customer accepts the deliverable

## Common SaaS Revenue Recognition Issues

### Annual Prepaid Subscriptions
Customer pays $1,200 upfront for annual access:
- Day 1: Cash DR $1,200, Deferred Revenue CR $1,200
- Month 1: Deferred Revenue DR $100, Revenue CR $100
- Repeat monthly for 12 months

### Mid-Period Upgrades
Customer upgrades from $100/mo to $200/mo on the 15th:
- For the current period: recognize ($100 × 0.5) + ($200 × 0.5) = $150
- From next period: recognize $200/mo

### Refunds and Cancellations
- If customer cancels and requests refund: reverse previously recognized revenue
- If customer cancels but no refund (standard SaaS terms): no reversal required, but stop recognizing future revenue

### Free Trials
- During free trial: no revenue (no contract, no consideration)
- When trial converts: revenue recognition begins from conversion date
