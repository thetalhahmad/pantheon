# Skill: Social Media Platform Strategy

## Platform Selection Framework

Do not be on every platform. Be excellent on 2, present on 1 more.

### LinkedIn — Primary for B2B
Best for: Reaching decision-makers, thought leadership, company credibility
Audience: Professionals, buyers, investors, talent
Content that works:
- Founder/executive opinions with a point of view
- Behind-the-scenes of building
- Customer stories and results (with numbers)
- Industry insights and hot takes
- Career milestones (team, funding, customers)

Does NOT work:
- Pure product promotion
- Corporate announcements without a human angle
- Stock photo + generic caption

### Twitter/X — For thought leadership and community
Best for: Real-time conversations, building a personal brand, connecting with other founders
Content that works:
- Short takes on industry news
- Threads sharing specific knowledge
- Building in public (metrics, decisions, failures)
- Engaging with others' content

### Instagram — Brand and culture (selective use)
Best for: Recruiting, humanizing the brand, visual products
Content that works:
- Team and office culture
- Product in use (screenshots of UI, if visually appealing)
- Event coverage
- Founder story content

## Content Strategy by Company Stage

### Pre-product / Early stage
Focus: Building credibility and audience before you need it
Content mix: 70% education/insights, 20% company story, 10% product direction
Goal: Build a following of potential customers, investors, and talent

### Post-launch / Growth stage
Content mix: 50% education, 30% social proof, 20% product
Goal: Convert audience members into trials and customers

### Scale stage
Content mix: 40% education, 30% customer success, 20% product, 10% company culture
Goal: Support sales, maintain brand, attract enterprise customers

## LinkedIn Algorithm Factors (2025)
- Dwell time matters more than likes
- Comments from relevant accounts carry more weight than from random accounts
- Native content (posts, documents, videos) over external links
- Polls and questions generate high engagement
- Optimal length: 150-300 words for text posts
- Posting time: Tuesday-Thursday 8-10am local time for business audiences
- Commenting on others' posts in the first hour boosts your post's reach

## Content Calendar Structure

### Weekly cadence (3-4 posts/week LinkedIn)
- Monday: Insight or opinion on industry topic
- Wednesday: Company/product/customer story
- Friday: More personal/human content (week in review, team highlight)
- Additional: Reactive content to news or conversations as relevant

### Monthly planning
- Week 1: Plan content for the month aligned to company milestones
- Week 2-3: Execute
- Week 4: Review analytics, identify what worked, adjust next month's plan

## Measuring What Matters

### Vanity metrics (track but do not optimize for)
- Followers, impressions, likes

### Signal metrics (these actually predict pipeline)
- Profile visits after a post (someone saw it and wanted to know more)
- Connection requests from ICP profiles
- DMs from potential customers referencing content
- Inbound inquiries that mention "I saw your post about..."

### Platform analytics to review monthly
- Which posts drove the most profile visits?
- Which content types got shared (not just liked)?
- Follower growth by week
- Engagement rate by content type
