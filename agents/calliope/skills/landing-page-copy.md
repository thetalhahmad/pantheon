# Skill: Landing Page Copywriting

## The Hero Section

### Headline formula
Best performing patterns:
1. "[Outcome] for [Audience]": "AI-assisted radiology for high-volume imaging centres"
2. "The [category] that [benefit]": "The invoicing platform that accountants actually trust"
3. "[Do X] without [pain]": "Close month-end without spreadsheet archaeology"
4. "[Adjective] [noun] for [audience]": "Clinical-grade AI for radiology teams"

### Subheadline
One sentence that:
- Adds specificity the headline left out
- Answers "how?" or "why?"
- Does NOT repeat the headline in different words

### CTA Button
- Verb + outcome: "Start reviewing smarter" not "Get started"
- Or verb + low friction signal: "Try free for 14 days"
- Never: "Submit", "Click here", "Learn more" as primary CTA

### Social Proof in Hero
Best position: immediately under or beside the CTA
Formats that work:
- "Join 500+ radiology teams" (scale signal)
- "★★★★★ 4.9/5 from 200+ reviews" (quality signal)
- Logos of 4-5 recognizable customers (authority signal)

## The Problem Section
This section makes the prospect feel understood before pitching.

### Structure
1. Name the symptom: "Most radiology departments are managing AI tools that weren't designed for clinical workflows"
2. Name the consequence: "The result: radiologists waste time reviewing low-priority findings while high-priority cases wait"
3. Validate the pain: "You've probably tried [common workaround]. It works until it doesn't."
4. Transition: "There's a different way."

### Words that signal understanding (use these)
- "You've probably..."
- "You already know..."
- "The problem isn't [obvious thing], it's [real thing]"
- "Every [X] has dealt with..."

### Words that signal you don't understand (avoid these)
- "Many organizations face challenges with..."
- "In today's complex landscape..."
- "As the industry evolves..."

## Feature-Benefit-Proof Formula
Never list features alone. For every feature:

```
Feature: [What it is]
Benefit: [What it does for the user]
Proof: [Evidence it works]
```

Example:
- Feature: "Priority scoring for AI findings"
- Benefit: "Review the findings that matter most first, not last"
- Proof: "Hospital NordHealth reduced miss rate by 18% in Q1"

## FAQ Section
The FAQ should preemptively handle the objections that prevent conversion:
1. "Is this HIPAA/GDPR compliant?" — Yes, and here is proof
2. "How long does implementation take?" — Specific timeframe
3. "Does this integrate with our PACS/EHR?" — List specific integrations
4. "What happens to our data?" — Clear, honest answer
5. "What if we want to cancel?" — No lock-in statement

FAQ = objection handling disguised as helpfulness.
